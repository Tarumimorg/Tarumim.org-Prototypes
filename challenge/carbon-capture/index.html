Capturing carbon on the scale that can make a difference to global warming requires a fundamental shift in how we engage with nature.


In any future scenario worth enduring, large scale reforestation will be key to the regeneration of the land and the atmosphere. Syntropic farms are highly efficient carbon sinks, working at maximum efficiency after 10 years. Each hectare absorbs:


1-3 years: 4.5 tonnes of carbon

4-9 years: 6.7 tonnes

10-15 years: 43 tonnes


By incentivising sustainable reforestation and raising the profile of the Syntropic Agriculture system in rural Brazil, Tarumim is part of a multifaceted global community of activists finding ways to make a meaningful impact on global warming.


Your personal Carbon footprint


The average Brit’s carbon footprint is about 10 tonnes per year.


Calculations are necessarily simplifications, but assuming that a tree absorbs around 0.5 kg per year, the average Brit should plant 2000 trees to render their life carbon neutral.

That equates to about 1-2 hectares.


Including the well we sank already, we calculate that our first pilot will cost roughly £5000.

It will reforest 7 hectares of land, with around 1-2 hectares comprising a fifth of this area. That results in a figure of £1000 per person to balance their carbon output.


Americans, with a national average that is twice this at around 20 tonnes per year, would need to invest £2000 into reforestation to offset their carbon output.
