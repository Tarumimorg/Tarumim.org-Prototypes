No Poverty

1

With the high yields of Syntropic Agriculture, people need not choose between Prosperity and Sustainability. Economic and Ecological health go together.


End poverty in all its forms everywhere

Sustainable Development Goals


From the local to the global, reforestation with Syntropic Agroforestry impacts the local and global. Forests rebalance the water cycle and absorb carbon as they grow, while populations of native species grow within them including various animals on the endangered list. The projects Tarumim supports in their initial phases soon provide financial independence for those working on them and food security for people living nearby.


Good Health and Well-Being

As well as providing medicine, trees and plants clean up pollution, reduce ambient temperatures and improve well-being, and the things in the forest are far more fascinating for children and adults alike than coffee plantations.

Zero Hunger

High yields contribute to food security. Syntropic Agriculture also improves the mineral and organic content of the soil much more than fertilizers do, meaning that agricultural produce is more nutritious.


End hunger, achieve food security and improved nutrition, promote sustainable agriculture

Achieve gender equality and empower all women and girls

5

Small-scale agriculture is often the province of women, who may be unable to go out to work all day because of family responsibilities. Many sell their own produce locally, which gives them financial independence and a degree of gender equality.

Clean Water and Sanitation

Scarcity of water is one of the major threats facing people in the regions. Tarumim’s projects are designed to manage this most precious resource.


Ensure access to water and sanitation for all

6

Affordable and Clean Energy

Solar panels are among the incentives offered to landowners in return for reforesting a portion of their land, and will also be incorporated into sustainable dwellings that are built for workers on our projects.

Ensure access to affordable, reliable, sustainable and modern energy for all

7

Decent Work and Economic Growth

Syntropic Argroforestry is more labour intensive than either monoculture farming or other reforestation programmes, providing good opportunities for employment. The high yields make wages competitive, and working shaded among the trees is more comfortable than working in the sun. Many would envy such employment.


Promote inclusive and sustainable economic growth, employment and decent work for all

8

Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure

Syntropic Argroforestry is a set of highly innovative methods of farming that are extremely beneficial to people on a local and a global scale. This young science is constantly adapting to novel environments, so further innovations are happening all the time that will be shared through our networks.


Build resilient infrastructure, promote sustainable industrialisation and foster innovation

9

Reduced Inequalities

The inequality in Brazil is stark, such that many people with small plots have little option but to rent out their land to giant cattle farming concerns. Innovative and lucrative agricultural practices don’t require expensive machinery or fertilizers, and can reduce inequality at the local level. Tarumim also has a program to acquire land to be stewarded by landless farmers until they are in a position to purchase it.


Reduce inequality within and among countries

10

Sustainable Cities and Communities

To a great extent, crime is a function of poor economic opportunities - one might think of many crimes as misguided acts of entrepreneurialism. Tarumim projects give employment and dignity to their labourers. Partners include rehab centres who can offer recovering addicts work regenerating the land as they regenerate their lives.


Make cities inclusive, safe, resilient and sustainable

11

Responsible Consumption and Production

Syntropic Agroforestry is better than sustainable - it is regenerative, improving the land while producing food for consumption.


Ensure sustainable consumption and production patterns

12

Climate Action

One hectare of new forest in this region of Brazil can absorb 100 tonnes of Carbon as it grows to maturity, while repurposing land that may have been used for cattle production, which produces a great deal of greenhouse gas.


Take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts

13

Life Below Water

Where it still exists the Atlantic Forest creates habitats at the coast that support marine biodiversity. It also slows down precipitation on its journey to the sea, reducing silting and erosion.


Conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas and marine resources

14

Life on Land

Syntropic Agroforestry is sustainable and regenerative, producing environments where flora, fauna and mankind can thrive. Our network will spread the greenery as they expand.


Sustainably manage forests, combat desertification, halt and reverse land degradation, halt biodiversity loss
