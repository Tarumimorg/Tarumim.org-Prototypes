Boa Vista Pilot Project

Project Background

In 2008, one of the founders of Tarumim visited São Sebastião da Boa Vista, a tiny village in one of the poorer parts of Brazil, and swam in a beautiful stream nearby. By 2015 the stream had dried up completely. It has always been dry in the valley of Jequtiona, but recently the dry season has grown longer each year.


People in Boa Vista understand the link between deforestation and the water cycle, because the eldest among them watched the devastation unfold. They talk about the time when there were more streams, and they were clean and full of water and wildlife, but often they lack the means to do much about it. From the perspective of conventional agriculture, reforesting land is a noble sacrifice that requires time, effort and money, and the reforested land is put out of production.


With poor employment opportunities, many of the men cut sugar cane far from home for nine months of the year to provide for their families, leaving their land for cattle or monoculture. Renting land for grazing provides a steady income. A eucalyptus plantation grows quickly and can easily be sold for charcoal, but it is also one of the most ecologically damaging crops, a forest without insects or birds that rapidly depletes underground water reserves and minerals.


Dona Rosarinha and Sr. Enrique own the land in question. They want to reforest 7 hectares that have been rented out for cattle grazing, and expand the thin strip of vegetation on either side of a stream that currently only flows when it rains. We have already sponsored a well that was dug in September, and they have been preparing saplings to plant for when we arrange a workshop on their land early in 2018.

Opportunity

Why plant trees?

Boa Vista has an active, cooperative community of people who help each other with their harvests and co-invest in machinery together. Manpower is cheap, readily available and enthusiastic. News spreads quickly. A project providing work, income and - after a time - high yields of produce could encourage other land-owners to consider something similar, either with our assistance or independently.

Project Goals

Our pilot project involves Research as well as Regeneration. We have identified the following short term goals to make the Boa Vista project a platform for learning about land restoration using Syntropic Agroforestry.

Sink a Well

Conduct a Syntropic Agroforestry Workshop

Initiate Agroforestry Programme over 3-7 Hectares

The project will be supported by regular visits from an agroforestry specialist, who will also present the methodology to other people in the co-operative.

Our measurement priorities are outlined below. For this pilot project we prioritise waterworks and ecosystem development over trees, learn why.

Tree cover

Rainfall and river flow

Agricultural productivity

Biodiversity

Soil quality

Projected impact

Sao Sebastiao da Boa Vista is like many places in rural Brazil: pretty much everyone talks to everyone about everyone and everything. People are curious about visitors and innovative projects, and information travels quickly. People also remember a time when the rivers were clean, full of water and wildlife.


We expect that in the initial phase other members of the community will be interested, taking part in the first workshop and follow-up visits from the agroforestry teacher, possibly considering something similar for their own land.


In the middle phase, as trees grow and the soil is regenerated, the dried up water spring will start to flow again. Dona Rosarinha will sell the produce generated in the square, and boast about how productive her land is. Owners of larger plots of land will initiate projects of their own, generating employment for the people of the village.


In the established phase, after seven years, the trees will be lush and chattering with birds and monkeys. The high productivity of Syntropic Agriculture will be obvious to people of the town, who will be employing the principles on their own land. Our research will have shown increased biodiversity, soil vitality and river flow.

Syntropic Agroforestry Workshops

Minas Gerais has a number of farms where Syntropic Agroforestry is practiced and taught. Tarumim supports rural communities and co-operatives that are interested in hosting workshops on their own farms, where people can learn the basics and begin to implement economically lucrative reforestation projects.


Workshops arrangement

Pilot program overarching goal is to establish a research platform for agroforestry tools, methods and economic models. Workshops play the key role, allowing us to learn from the community, exchange knowledge with people engaged in land restoration and reforestation, agroforestry.


Our initial tasks are:


Step 1: Two people from a community will spend three days on a syntropic farm.

Step 2: A teacher will visit their community and present a workshop to interested local people.

Step 3: A small network of teachers will travel between enrolled and interested farms, providing ongoing instruction and assistance.


The teachers are Brazilians expert Syntropic Agroforestry practitioners who will maintain ongoing relationships with the projects as they develop, moving saplings and other materials between them.


We are preparing cost structure materials and will publish those soon.


Workshop training will include

General introduction to the principles of Syntropic Agriculture

Specific principles relevant to the land in question

Successionary planting - what to plant and when as the land regenerates

Fruit, vegetable and grain production

Management of ecosystems and water resources
