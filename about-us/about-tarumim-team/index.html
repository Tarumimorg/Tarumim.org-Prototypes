We are a small group of people who think planting trees is one of the most meaningful activities in the 21st century. We rely on collective wisdom and work to empower crowdsourcing of grassroots reforestation initiatives. Through experimentation we aim to develop ideas with good scalability and high positive social impact potential. We are in the early step of team building, both in London, UK and in various parts of Minas Gerais, Brazil.

Dr. Sam Gandy

Sam’s lifelong love of wildlife and the outdoors inspired his academic research into physical geography, insects and ecology. In the past he worked with ecological river restoration at the University of Leicester, and he recently completed his PhD in ecological sciences at The James Hutton Institute and the University of Aberdeen, conducting research in southern Ethiopia on termites, soil-water dynamics and soil degradation and regeneration. He is devoting himself to ecological restoration.


Danny Diskin

Danny is interested in the regenerative powers of nature, both in terms of how land recovers its biodiversity after land degradation, and how forests improve the physical, psychological and economic health of the people who learn their secrets. He spent nearly three years in Brazil, both in the Amazon and in Minas Gerais. His background is in teaching, writing and hypnotherapy, his future is in the trees.

Thiago Senra

Bioconstruction engineer, bamboo artisan and gardener, Thiago’s bamboo designs began with wind chimes and have progressed to two storey sustainable structures with integrated with solar panels and water collection. He is helping co-ordinate projects on the Brazilian side.

Vladimiras Lekecinskas

Entrepreneur, software projects manager, designer and a dreamer, Vlad joined Tarumim team in search for a meaningful and yet simple and transparent reality impact model. Planting trees makes sense, is well understood, simple to measure, study and compare. Tree cover restoration as a carbon capture tool helping us slow down (and reverse) global warming - could work.

Mae das Aguas

In the Brazilian Amazon, stories are told about Tarumim. She is the Mother of the Waters and the Bringer of the Rains. One story recalls a man who called upon her three times during a drought, and it rained so hard that his roof tiles were washed away. Tarumim is a spirit who shouldn’t be messed with.
