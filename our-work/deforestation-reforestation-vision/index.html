Regenerating Forests

Forests provide a wealth of benefits for humans and millions of other species, locking up carbon and driving down the temperature while regulating the water cycle and fortifying communities against the effects of climate change.


Causes of Deforestation


Forest cover has nearly halved since the dawn of human civilisation, mostly in the last 300 years.


The Atlantic forest is among the most biologically rich set of biomes in the world, with dry regions and moist regions and mangroves where the rivers meet the sea. It covered 1 million square km back when the region was unnamed.


Any reasonable attempt at navigating our collective future is going to involve reforestation on a massive scale.


Once upon a time, the only people to take an interest in the forests of southeastern Brazil were were slave-raiders plundering the indigenous populations, but things changed with the discovery of gold in 1690. Prospectors dammed up streams to access riverbeds, and blasted river banks clean of vegetation to prospect among the mud. The forest was burned for centuries, for mining and to flush out pockets of resistance from Indians and escaped black slaves. The population swelled, and the forest was razed for pasture to feed it beef and cheese. Grazing quickly degrades the land, and there was always more forest to clear, so exhausted plots were soon abandoned and left to hardy exotic grasses to take over.


The Situation Today


MG has lost 60% of its native vegetation and over 90% of its Atlantic forest, and what remains is still under threat from logging and monoculture. While many rural people would like to preserve the forests they grew up enjoying, economic necessities trap them into a cycle of diminishing returns. The consequences of deforestation are visible from space in the pale colour of the land, and often in the sparseness of cloud cover above. The water cycle is dependent on the health of the forest, and our future depends on the water cycle.


Tarumim’s mission is to incentivise Reforestation and support people doing it.


Bringing back the forest can create abundance and resilience, mitigating some of the problems facing rural people. Given the right support, the forest and its wildlife rapidly regenerate.
